Data Practices & Privacy

You shouldn't have to guess how your data is used. Here's exactly what Tether collects, what we never touch, and how your group's information stays protected.

"You don't have to do this alone — and your data doesn't have to be exposed to get support."

Native Framework Usage

Tether is built on Apple's own privacy-first frameworks. Your restriction settings never leave your device, and we only receive what Apple explicitly allows.

  • FamilyControls
    Authorization only. Used to authorize Tether as a screen time management tool. All authorization happens through Apple's secure system prompts — we never see your Apple ID or credentials.
  • ManagedSettings
    Device-local enforcement. App blocking is applied directly on your device. Restriction rules are never transmitted to our servers — the enforcement happens entirely on-device.
  • DeviceActivity
    Aggregate totals only. We receive summary data only — for example, "45 minutes of Social" — never a log of which specific apps were opened or when. This powers your group's usage reports.

What We Collect vs. What We Don't

Tether is built on the principle of data minimization. We only collect what's required for accountability features to work — nothing more.

Category What We Collect What We Never See
App Usage Daily aggregate totals by category Specific apps opened, timestamps, or session content
Communications None Contacts, call logs, or message content
Browsing Category filter status only Specific URLs, search queries, or browser history
Location None GPS data, current location, or movement history
Unlock Requests App category, reason, timestamp Screenshots or content of the requested app
Group Data Member list, approval decisions Private messages between members outside the app

Secure Cloud Synchronization

Tether syncs group configurations and approval history across devices so your accountability partners can respond in real time. Here's exactly what is stored in the cloud:

  • User accounts: Email address, display name, and hashed authentication credentials (via AWS Cognito). We never store passwords in plain text.
  • Group membership: Who is in your accountability group and their assigned roles (member or approver).
  • Restriction settings: Your selected list of restricted app categories and schedule preferences.
  • Request & approval log: An immutable record of unlock requests, approval decisions, and timestamps. This is the audit trail that keeps everyone honest.
  • Aggregate usage reports: Daily screen time summaries shared with your group — category totals only, not individual app logs.
Encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256) — hosted on AWS

Data Deletion

You own your data. You can request full deletion of your account and all associated records at any time — from within the app or by contacting our support team.

When you delete your account, all personal information, group associations, usage history, and request logs are permanently purged from our infrastructure within 30 days. Records shared with your accountability group are anonymized rather than deleted, to preserve the integrity of the shared audit log.
